16-QAM Specifications

Symbols
16
Bits per Symbol
4
I/Q Levels
4
Spectral Efficiency
4 bps/Hz
16-QAM encodes 4 bits per symbol using 4 amplitude levels on both I (in-phase) and Q (quadrature) axes, yielding 16 distinct constellation points. It is widely used in 802.11n/ac Wi-Fi, DVB-T, and LTE for moderate SNR conditions.
⚡ Higher-order QAM requires greater SNR. 4096-QAM needs ~33 dB SNR for acceptable BER, while 16-QAM works reliably at ~15 dB.
